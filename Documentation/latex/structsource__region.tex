\begin{DoxyRemark}{Remarks}
toml++\textquotesingle{}s parser is unicode-\/aware insofar as it knows how to handle non-\/\+ASCII whitespace and newline characters, but it doesn\textquotesingle{}t give much thought to combining marks, grapheme clusters vs. characters, et cetera. If a TOML document contains lots of codepoints outside of the ASCII range you may find that your source\+\_\+positions don\textquotesingle{}t match those given by a text editor (typically the line numbers will be accurate but column numbers will be too high). {\bfseries{This is not an error.}} I\textquotesingle{}ve chosen this behaviour as a deliberate trade-\/off between parser complexity and correctness. 
\end{DoxyRemark}
